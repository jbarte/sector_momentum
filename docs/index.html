How to read this tab

Every sector is ranked by composite score — a cross-sectional z-score blending level momentum (how strong the sector is now) and change momentum (whether it's accelerating or decelerating). A score of 0 means average across all sectors; +1.0 means one standard deviation above average.

Trend badge — the most actionable signal

Computed as the linear slope of rank over the last 3–5 scans. A falling rank number means the sector is climbing the leaderboard.

  • ↑↑ Rising fast — rank improving 1.5+ places per scan. Strong entry signal.
  • ↑ Rising — rank improving 0.3–1.5 places per scan. Emerging momentum.
  • → Flat — rank stable. Momentum is neither building nor unwinding.
  • ↓ Falling — rank deteriorating even if it is still high. Momentum is fading.
  • ↓↓ Falling fast — avoid or consider exiting.

Entry setup

Composite > 0 with ↑ or ↑↑ trajectory and a positive Change score. Click any row to see the full signal breakdown.

Exit / avoid signal

↓ or ↓↓ trajectory even when rank is still high — the sector is topping. Rank Δ turning negative confirms the reversal.

How to read this tab

Sectors are plotted by relative strength vs benchmark (x-axis) and whether that relative strength is accelerating or decelerating (y-axis). Both axes are centred at 100.

Four quadrants

  • Leading (top-right): Outperforming AND accelerating. Currently strong — but watch for rotation into Weakening.
  • Weakening (bottom-right): Outperforming but decelerating. Prime exit / reduce signal.
  • Lagging (bottom-left): Underperforming AND decelerating. Avoid.
  • Improving (top-left): Underperforming but accelerating. Prime entry signal — sectors typically rotate counter-clockwise from here into Leading.

How to use

Sectors rotate Leading → Weakening → Lagging → Improving → Leading. Buy in Improving before the move to Leading; sell in Weakening before the drop to Lagging. Dotted tail lines show the last 3 scan positions — the direction of the tail matters more than the current position.

Note: current axes are composite-based proxies. True JdK RS-Ratio/Momentum requires daily price data relative to a benchmark.

How to read this tab

Select a sector to see its score component history across all stored scans.

Key patterns

  • Composite rising + Change score rising: sustained acceleration. Highest-conviction entry setup.
  • Composite near peak + Change score turning negative: the sector is topping. Reduce or exit.
  • Composite falling but Change score rising: bottoming process. Watch for a second consecutive scan confirming before entering.
  • Level score and Change score diverging: momentum structure is breaking down — treat with caution.

How to use

Pair with the leaderboard Trend badge. A sector showing ↑ on the leaderboard that also has rising composite and change_score in the drill-down is the highest-conviction setup. A sector with a high rank but flat or falling change_score is likely to slip in the next scan.

How to read this tab

Composite score trend across all stored scans for every sector. The composite is a cross-sectional z-score — 0 = sector-average for that scan.

Key patterns

  • Consistent upward slope: quality momentum. The sector has been outperforming for multiple scans — this is the highest-quality signal.
  • Sharp spike then reversal: likely a one-time data event, not sustainable momentum. Treat with caution.
  • Gradual recovery from negative territory: potential emerging opportunity — cross-check the leaderboard Trend badge for confirmation.
  • Flat around zero: sector in line with average; no edge.

How to use

Use as context for current rank. A sector ranked #1 after steadily rising for 10 scans is a different risk profile from one that jumped from #8 last scan. Long, smooth uptrends are the entries worth holding; V-shaped reversals are the ones to trade quickly or skip.

Bottom-right quadrant (data ahead of sentiment) is the early-rotation zone. Hollow points = no sentiment data this scan (source unavailable or EU-only). Sentiment is a confirming signal — noisy, gameable, retail-biased. This is analytical tooling, not investment advice.

Leaderboard

The leaderboard ranks every sector by its composite score — a weighted blend of level, momentum-change, and data-quality sub-scores. A higher composite (more positive z-score) means the sector is exhibiting stronger relative momentum versus its benchmark.

  • Level score — captures the current absolute momentum level (returns, MA structure, OBV trend).
  • Change score — captures how momentum is accelerating or decelerating versus the prior scan.
  • Data score — reflects data completeness; sectors with missing signals are penalised.
  • Rank Δ — how many ranks the sector climbed (▲) or fell (▼) versus the previous scan.

Click any column header to sort the table.

Relative Rotation Graph (RRG)

The RRG plots sectors on two axes derived from their relative strength vs the benchmark index:

  • X-axis (RS-Ratio) — whether the sector is outperforming (> 100) or underperforming (< 100) the benchmark.
  • Y-axis (RS-Momentum) — whether relative strength is accelerating (> 100) or decelerating (< 100).

Quadrant interpretation:

  • Leading (top-right) — outperforming and accelerating.
  • Weakening (bottom-right) — outperforming but decelerating.
  • Lagging (bottom-left) — underperforming and decelerating.
  • Improving (top-left) — underperforming but accelerating.

Dotted tail lines show the last 3 scan positions, indicating rotation direction. Note: in Phase 1 the axes are proxies derived from composite score and rank; true JdK RS-Ratio values require the RRG signal data from the signals table.

Drill-down

The movers panel shows the rank change between the two most recent scans. Green bars indicate sectors that climbed the leaderboard; terracotta bars indicate sectors that slipped. Large moves may reflect data updates, macro events, or genuine momentum shifts.

History

A line chart of composite score across all stored scans. Persistent upward trends in a sector's composite score indicate sustained momentum, while reversals may signal rotation into or out of the sector.

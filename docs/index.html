Leaderboard

The Leaderboard ranks every sector by its composite score — a weighted blend of level, momentum-change, and data-quality sub-scores. A higher composite (more positive z-score) means the sector is exhibiting stronger relative momentum versus its benchmark.

  • Level score — captures the current absolute momentum level (returns, MA structure, OBV trend).
  • Change score — captures how momentum is accelerating or decelerating versus the prior scan.
  • Data score — reflects data completeness; sectors with missing signals are penalised.
  • Rank Δ — how many ranks the sector climbed (▲) or fell (▼) versus the previous scan.

Click any column header to sort the table.

Relative Rotation Graph (RRG)

The RRG plots sectors on two axes derived from their relative strength vs the benchmark index:

  • X-axis (RS-Ratio) — whether the sector is outperforming (> 100) or underperforming (< 100) the benchmark.
  • Y-axis (RS-Momentum) — whether relative strength is accelerating (> 100) or decelerating (< 100).

Quadrant interpretation:

  • Leading (top-right) — outperforming and accelerating.
  • Weakening (bottom-right) — outperforming but decelerating.
  • Lagging (bottom-left) — underperforming and decelerating.
  • Improving (top-left) — underperforming but accelerating.

Dotted tail lines show the last 3 scan positions, indicating rotation direction. Note: in Phase 1 the axes are proxies derived from composite score and rank; true JdK RS-Ratio values require the RRG signal data from the signals table.

Drill-down

Select a sector from the dropdown to view how each score component has evolved over time. Useful for spotting multi-scan trends — a rising change_score alongside a rising composite is a strong signal of sustained momentum.

Movers

The Movers panel shows the rank change between the two most recent scans. Blue bars indicate sectors that climbed the leaderboard; pink bars indicate sectors that slipped. Large moves may reflect data updates, macro events, or genuine momentum shifts.

History

A line chart of composite score across all stored scans. Persistent upward trends in a sector's composite score indicate sustained momentum, while reversals may signal rotation into or out of the sector.

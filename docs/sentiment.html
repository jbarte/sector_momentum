How is the sentiment score calculated?

Source — Google Trends only

Sentiment measures search attention using Google Trends. Reddit and news-headline sources were evaluated and dropped — no reliable free access.

Method

For each sector we pull ~13 weeks of worldwide search interest for its primary keyword (e.g. Technology → semiconductor, Energy → oil). The headline score is the trend (slope) of that interest, standardized across the 11 sectors (z-score): positive = rising attention vs peers, negative = fading. Where a ticker maps to a known Google Knowledge Graph entity, we query that entity rather than the raw ticker string, to avoid name collisions (e.g. a ticker that is also a common word); tickers without a known entity fall back to the plain string. Interest is pulled per region — US sectors in US search, EU sectors averaged across Germany, France and the UK — and normalized against a neutral, ubiquitous anchor term rather than a market ticker. (This anchor change means sentiment values from before it are not directly comparable to later ones.)

Derived signals

From the same interest series we read out a few complementary views (shown in the table below, information-only — only Momentum can affect the ranking):

  • Momentum — the trend/slope, same series the headline score is built from.
  • Acceleration — recent-half slope minus earlier-half slope; positive means attention is picking up speed.
  • Range position — where the latest reading sits within its own 13-week min–max (0% = window low, 100% = window high).
  • Spike — z-score of the latest point vs the trailing weeks; a breakout in interest.
  • Volatility — how choppy week-to-week interest has been.

It does not drive the ranking by default

The leaderboard composite is built from price/momentum signals only. Sentiment changes the ranking only when you enable the Sentiment toggle, at the weight you choose. Treat it as a confirmer, not a driver — search interest is noisy and can lag price.

Bottom-right quadrant (data ahead of sentiment) is the early-rotation zone. Hollow points = no sentiment data this scan (source unavailable or EU-only). Sentiment is a confirming signal — noisy, gameable, retail-biased. This is analytical tooling, not investment advice.

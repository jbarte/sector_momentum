How is the sentiment score calculated?

Source — FinBERT news sentiment

The sentiment score is built from recent financial news. For each of the 11 GICS sectors we fetch the latest 24 hours of English-language headlines from the GDELT global news index (up to 250 per sector, matched via sector-specific topic codes). Each headline is scored by ProsusAI/finbert, a finance-tuned BERT model that classifies text as positive, negative or neutral with a confidence score. The per-sector mean signed polarity (positive = +score, negative = −score, neutral = 0) is then z-scored across the 11 sectors: positive = more bullish news vs peers, negative = more bearish. Both US and EU rows for the same GICS sector share the same score — financial news sentiment is global. If FinBERT or GDELT is unavailable, the score is left blank for that scan.

Table columns

  • Polarity — mean signed sentiment from recent news headlines. Green = bullish, red = bearish.
  • Articles — number of headlines scored.
  • Pos% — fraction classified as positive.
  • Neg% — fraction classified as negative.

It does not drive the ranking by default

The leaderboard composite is built from price/momentum signals only. Sentiment changes the ranking only when you enable the Sentiment toggle, at the weight you choose. Treat it as a confirmer, not a driver — news sentiment is noisy and can lag price.

Bottom-right quadrant (data ahead of sentiment) is the early-rotation zone. Hollow points = no news sentiment this scan (source unavailable). Sentiment is a confirming signal — noisy and news-driven. This is analytical tooling, not investment advice.

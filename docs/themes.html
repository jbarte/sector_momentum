How to read this tab

Themes are plotted by relative strength vs benchmark (x-axis) and whether that relative strength is accelerating or decelerating (y-axis). Both axes are centred at 100.

Four quadrants

  • Leading (top-right): Outperforming AND accelerating.
  • Weakening (bottom-right): Outperforming but decelerating. Prime exit / reduce signal.
  • Lagging (bottom-left): Underperforming AND decelerating. Avoid.
  • Improving (top-left): Underperforming but accelerating. Prime entry signal.

How to use

Themes rotate Leading → Weakening → Lagging → Improving → Leading. Buy in Improving before the move to Leading; sell in Weakening before the drop to Lagging. Dotted tail lines show the last scan positions — the direction of the tail matters more than the current position.

How to read this tab

Select a theme to see its score component history across all stored scans.

Key patterns

  • Composite rising + Change score rising: sustained acceleration. Highest-conviction entry setup.
  • Composite near peak + Change score turning negative: the theme is topping. Reduce or exit.
  • Composite falling but Change score rising: bottoming process. Watch for confirmation before entering.

Key patterns

  • Consistent upward slope: quality momentum sustained over multiple scans.
  • Sharp spike then reversal: likely a one-time event, not sustainable.
  • Gradual recovery from negative territory: potential emerging opportunity.

Leaderboard

The leaderboard ranks every thematic ETF by its composite score — a weighted blend of level, momentum-change, and data-quality sub-scores measured against a global benchmark (ACWI). A higher composite means the theme is exhibiting stronger relative momentum.

  • Level score — current absolute momentum level (returns, MA structure, OBV trend).
  • Change score — how momentum is accelerating or decelerating versus the prior scan.
  • Data score — data completeness; themes with missing signals are penalised.
  • Rank Δ — how many ranks the theme climbed (▲) or fell (▼) versus the previous scan.

Click any row to see the full signal breakdown and UCITS alternative instruments.

Relative Rotation Graph (RRG)

The RRG plots themes on two axes derived from their relative strength vs the benchmark:

  • X-axis (RS-Ratio) — whether the theme is outperforming (> 100) or underperforming (< 100) the benchmark.
  • Y-axis (RS-Momentum) — whether relative strength is accelerating (> 100) or decelerating (< 100).

Dotted tail lines show the last scan positions per theme, indicating rotation direction. Themes rotating from Improving → Leading are the strongest early-trend setups.

Drill-down

Select a theme from the dropdown to view how each score component has evolved over time. A rising change_score alongside a rising composite signals sustained momentum.

Movers

Rank change between the two most recent scans. Green bars indicate themes that climbed; terracotta bars indicate themes that slipped.

History

A line chart of composite score across all stored scans. Persistent upward trends indicate sustained momentum; reversals may signal rotation into or out of the theme.

Backtest

Monthly top-N rotation backtest: at each month-end the scanner scores all themes using only price data available up to that date (no look-ahead), picks the top N, and holds them equal-weight for the next month. The equity curve compares the strategy to the ACWI benchmark. Metrics: CAGR, Sharpe (risk-free = 0%), maximum drawdown, hit rate (% of months the strategy beat the benchmark), and average monthly turnover. Run python backtest.py to regenerate.

Getting started

Sector Momentum

This dashboard tracks relative momentum across the 11 GICS sectors for both US (SPDR ETFs) and EU (STOXX 600). Automated daily scans rank sectors by momentum strength, detect trend shifts, and surface entry and exit setups.

Three pages — Sectors, Themes, and Sentiment — accessible from the top navigation. Let’s walk through what each one shows you.

Sectors page

The Leaderboard

Your home screen. Every sector is ranked by composite score — a cross-sectional z-score blending:

  • Level — current momentum strength (returns, MA structure, OBV trend)
  • Change — whether momentum is accelerating or decelerating
  • Data — signal completeness; sectors with gaps are penalized

Zero means average across sectors; +1.0 is one standard deviation above. Click any row for the full signal breakdown.

Most actionable signal

Trend Badges

Computed from rank slope over the last 3–5 scans. A falling rank number means the sector is climbing the leaderboard.

  • ↑↑ Rising fast — rank improving 1.5+ places/scan
  • ↑ Rising — emerging momentum (0.3–1.5 places)
  • → Flat — no directional signal
  • ↓ Falling — momentum fading, even at high rank
  • ↓↓ Falling fast — avoid or consider exiting
Entry setup: Composite > 0 with ↑ or ↑↑ and a positive Change score. Exit: ↓ or ↓↓ even at a high rank — the sector is topping.
RRG tab

Relative Rotation

Sectors plotted on two axes: relative strength vs. benchmark (x) and whether that strength is accelerating (y). Sectors rotate counter-clockwise through four quadrants:

  • Leading (top-right) — outperforming and accelerating
  • Weakening (bottom-right) — outperforming but decelerating
  • Lagging (bottom-left) — underperforming and decelerating
  • Improving (top-left) — the entry zone
Key: Buy in Improving before the move to Leading. Sell in Weakening before the drop to Lagging. The dotted tail shows the last 3 scans — direction matters more than position.
Drill-down, Movers, History & Backtest

Going Deeper

Drill-down — select a sector to see its score components across all stored scans. Rising composite + rising Change = sustained acceleration (highest-conviction entry). High composite + falling Change = the sector is topping.

Movers — rank change between the two most recent scans. Large single-scan jumps need Drill-down confirmation before acting.

History — all sectors' composite trends over time. Long, smooth uptrends are the entries worth holding; V-shaped reversals are the ones to trade quickly or skip.

Backtest — monthly top-5 rotation strategy vs benchmark, with CAGR, Sharpe, max drawdown, and hit rate. Validates whether the momentum signals would have generated edge historically.

Sentiment page

Search Attention

Google Trends–based search interest, pulled per region (US direct, EU averaged across DE/FR/GB). Six derived signals per sector:

  • Momentum — trend slope of search interest
  • Acceleration — is the trend speeding up?
  • Range position — current vs. 13-week min/max
  • Spike & Volatility — breakout detection and choppiness
  • Attention — head-to-head comparison across all sectors in the same region
Info-only by default. Enable the Sentiment toggle on the leaderboard to blend it into composite rankings at the weight you choose.
